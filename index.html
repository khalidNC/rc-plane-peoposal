Affordable RC Model Aircraft Made in Bangladesh

Low-cost, locally manufactured RC planes for education and hobbyists

Potential National Applications

Agriculture Monitoring

Monitoring large crop fields, observing irrigation patterns, and assessing crop conditions.

Crop Spraying Concept (Future Scope)

Conceptual use for precision agriculture such as pesticide or fertilizer spraying (future development).

Disaster & Flood Monitoring

Large Area & Rural Monitoring

Monitoring fish farms, rural land, and large open areas efficiently at low cost.

Introduction

This project presents a locally developed Radio-Controlled (RC) model aircraft built using lightweight materials such as Depron foam and balsa wood, combined with commercially available electronic components. The purpose of this project is to demonstrate the feasibility of designing and producing affordable RC aircraft within Bangladesh.

Objective

  • To showcase a fully functional RC model aircraft
  • To demonstrate cost-effective local production capability
  • To explore opportunities in education, hobby, and toy markets

Problem Statement

Currently, most RC model aircraft available in Bangladesh are imported and expensive, making them inaccessible to a large number of students and hobbyists. This limits creativity, hands-on learning, and innovation in this field. Additionally, there is little to no local manufacturing in this segment.

Proposed Solution

This project introduces a low-cost, locally manufactured RC aircraft that:

  • Uses easily available materials (Depron foam, balsa wood)
  • Can be assembled using basic tools and skills
  • Maintains reliable flight performance
  • Costs significantly less than imported alternatives

Demonstration Plan

At the ICT Festival, the following will be presented:

  • A fully built RC model aircraft
  • A partially assembled model to demonstrate structure and design
  • Key components such as motor, ESC, receiver, and propeller
  • Cost comparison between imported and locally built models
  • A live or recorded flight demonstration (if feasible)

Future Scope

  • DIY RC aircraft kits for students and beginners
  • Ready-to-fly models for hobbyists
  • STEM-based training workshops in schools and universities
  • Small-scale local manufacturing setup
  • Potential export to similar emerging markets

Expected Impact

  • Promote STEM education and hands-on learning
  • Encourage local innovation and technical skills development
  • Reduce dependency on imported hobby products
  • Create opportunities for entrepreneurship and small-scale manufacturing
  • Inspire youth interest in aviation and engineering

Support Required

  • Initial funding for prototyping and production
  • Access to innovation labs or incubation centers
  • Collaboration with educational institutions and training programs

Conclusion

This project can evolve from a low-cost RC aircraft into an affordable aerial monitoring platform suitable for agriculture, disaster response, and large-area observation. Its key advantage lies in low cost, local manufacturability, and adaptability, making it a practical solution for scalable deployment in Bangladesh.
